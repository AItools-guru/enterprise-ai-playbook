Organizations often struggle to quantify the actual financial impact of AI. This simulator models the real-world operational savings of deploying specialized prompts across a team to automate repetitive daily tasks (like writing PRDs, cargo risk logs, and database indexes).

What is an Enterprise Global Node?

A Global Node represents a geographically distributed, highly specialized AI execution hub deployed within our corporate network. Each node runs customized Large Language Models (LLMs) tuned specifically for local department workflows (e.g., Supply Chain in Seattle, Software Engineering in Austin).

1. Specialized Agents

Running targeted system prompts to automate critical workflows like safety stock modeling, story splitting, or database refactoring.

2. Dynamic Financial Sync

Moving the global team sliders on the ROI Simulator dynamically calculates the net savings and distributes them across active hubs based on their ROI Weights.

3. Live Telemetry

Simulates active telemetry feedback streams, creating minor periodic fluctuations in latency metrics and operational velocity in real-time.

4. Cross-Model Deployment

Demonstrates multi-model infrastructure using Llama-3-Agent, Claude-3.5-Sonnet, and GPT-4o-Coder aligned with specialized regional duties.
